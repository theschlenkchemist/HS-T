01
Production Process
99% lower emissions per carat than conventional diamond CVD
Plain English

We grow diamond using CO₂ and e-methane instead of hydrogen, powered entirely by solar from Year 1. The conventional industry uses fossil hydrogen and grid electricity. That difference accounts for almost all of the gap. Our lower reaction temperature also means we use 67% less electrical energy than the state of the art.

02
Use-Phase Efficiency · Largest Impact
1,087 kgCO₂e avoided per GPU per year
Equivalent to taking a car off the road — for every chip we coat
Plain English

Diamond is the best thermal conductor on earth. Coating a chip with it keeps the device cooler, so it uses less energy, runs more efficiently, and lasts longer. Each of those three effects reduces carbon emissions. Cooler chips also need less energy to cool them down, saving further energy at the data centre level.

03
Permanent Sequestration
3.66 kgCO₂ permanently locked per kg of diamond produced
Plain English

The carbon in our diamond starts as CO₂ captured from the atmosphere. Once it becomes diamond, it cannot decompose or release back into the air. It is permanently removed from the carbon cycle — locked in a crystal lattice that is chemically inert and indefinitely stable.
